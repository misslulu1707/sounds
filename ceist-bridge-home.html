○ Where both say the same thing: Vedic Moon in Rohini and the Western Solstice both counsel tending over launching. Rohini favours slow consolidation; the Solstice is a hinge between expansion and inward turn. Today is not for forcing new beginnings.
Where they read differently
◇ The Moon: Vedic reads your Moon as Taurus (Rohini) — earthy, rooted, slow. Western reads it as Gemini — quick, idea-driven, restless. Both are true; they describe different layers of you. Your Vedic Moon is your karmic emotional pattern. Your Western Moon is your psychological emotional pattern.
◇ Saturn retrograde in Western: Saturn is currently retrograding through Pisces in the tropical system — affecting your H2 (finances, voice). Vedic doesn't read Saturn retrograde the same way: in Jyotish, retrograde (vakri) Saturn often becomes stronger and more exacting, not delayed. The Western reading softens; the Vedic intensifies.
◇ The Solstice: Western astrology gives the Solstice enormous seasonal weight — it's a cardinal ingress, a moment of maximum solar power. Vedic does not track it; the sidereal calendar operates independently of seasons. This is not a flaw in either system — they are literally measuring different cosmological clocks.
